The No-Drill Implant Procedure

  1. Any unrestorable tooth necessitating an extraction.
  2. The Osteoconverter™ is threaded into the socket, acting as both an osteotome and bone condenser.
  3. The socket is prepared for an implant three ways:
    1. Converts socket curves and irregularities into a suitable implant site
    2. Scores the internal socket wall in 1mm increments providing optimal blood flow for implant healing
    3. Expands bone to provide ideal initial implant stability (45Ncm to 65Ncm on average)
  4. The Osteoconverter™ can also act as an implant. It may be left in the socket as the final implant,  or the clinician can place an appropriately sized implant of their choosing.

The 1-Drill Implant Procedure Using the Multi-Drill™

  1. Traditional multiple drill sequence for osteotomy not needed.
  2. Single hole with Multi-Drill™ completes entire process.
  3. Customizable abutment being attached to the implant.
  4. Prep abutment and refine the margins before moving on to the final impression.
  5. Implant insertion completed using the 3 in 1 implant procedure.

The OsteoHybrid™ Implant Procedure

  1. The apex of the OsteoHybrid™ is mini sized at 1.8 mm, while the occlusal portion is a conventional 3.75 mm.
  2. The OsteoHybrid™ implant allows for just one drill, acting as a bone condenser to make the ridge accommodate the implant.
  3. Works like an osteotome in an efficient manner through its self-tapping design.

The OsteoLift™ Procedure

  1. Implant prepared to enter osteotomy site.
  2. OsteoLift™ gently bumps sinus without perforating it.
